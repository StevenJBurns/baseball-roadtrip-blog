Friday, Apr. 6 10:05pm ET
Angels spoil A's home opener

 

OAKLAND, Calif. (AP) – Ben Molina kept in mind his father's advice.

 

 

Molina

"My dad once said if you hit the ball, you have a chance to get a hit. If you swing and miss, there is no chance," he said. "I try to put the ball in play, and see if it will find a hole."

Molina went 2-for-4, driving in three runs as the Anaheim Angels spoiled the Oakland Athletics' home opener with a 5-4 victory.

Matt Wise (1-0) struck out a career-high seven in six innings, allowing four runs and six hits.

"He used his fastball aggressively, which set the tone for his changeup," manager Mike Scioscia said. "He challenged guys inside and changed speeds very effectively."

Troy Percival pitched the ninth for his first save as Anaheim even its record at 2-2 after opening with two losses.

Molina's bases-loaded single in the fourth scored Troy Glaus and Garret Anderson. Left fielder Johnny Damon's throwing error allowed Scott Spiezio and Molina to advance on the play, and they scored on David Eckstein's drive down the left-field line to give the Angels a 4-1 lead.

Oakland closed to 4-3 in the bottom half on Olmedo Saenz's two-run homer.

Molina, whose father was a top amateur player in Puerto Rico, added an RBI single in the sixth that scored Glenallen Hill.

Eric Chavez hit a run-scoring single in the bottom half.

Oakland loaded the bases in the seventh against Shigetoshi Hasegawa with one out, but Jose Ortiz hit into a double play.

"That one hurt," A's manager Art Howe said.

Mark Mulder (0-1) allowed five runs -- four earned -- and seven hits in 5 1/3 innings.

"They hit some good pitches," Mulder said. "They weren't quite as good as they should have been, I guess."

Oakland, which made the playoffs last year but was eliminated by the New York Yankees in the first round, drew 42,826 for its homer opener, about 1,000 short of capacity.

Jason Giambi was greeted by chants of "MVP! MVP!" when he stepped up to bat in the first inning. Giambi answered with a single that scored Damon from second and finished the night going 3-for-4. Giambi is batting .417 for the A's, who have won just one of their first four games.

"We had our opportunities to win the game, even with our lack of consistent offense, if you want to call it that," Howe said. "It was there for the taking. We just didn't stand up and take it."

It had rained off and on throughout the day in Oakland, and puddles formed on the Coliseum's warning track. A light drizzle fell in the third inning, but subsided by the fourth.

The periodic rain and cool temperatures kept the crowd rather subdued for the home opener, but there was a loud cheer when the Yankees' 13-4 loss to Toronto was posted on the scoreboard.
