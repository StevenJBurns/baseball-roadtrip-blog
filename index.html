Welcome to my chunk of cyber-space dedicated to a thing I am calling Baseball Roadtrip 2001. I've been a fan of America's Pastime since I was five years old and over time I've become an all around fan and a purist  "for love of the game" as they say.  I will always root for the New York Mets when given the chance however!  Being a fan is a 25 year work in progress and I probably always will be until I see my very last game (or there's another ridiculous strike).

Roadtrip is an idea I've had for nearly nine years since two college friends drove across the country stopping to see a handful of major league games in several cities back in 1992.  Since then I've tinkered with the idea of seeing a game in every major league ballpark around the country (including Canada!).  Time, money, circumstances, procrastination, personal and professional obligations have kept me from starting out on this adventure but now it might just happen.  The ambitious plan is to put a check mark next to every ballpark on the list over the course of just one season.  The reality will probably be that I can't complete a tour of 30 ballparks in just six months in which case it will be a fluid thing.  My busy schedule for the summer of 2001 already looks to conspire against me...

A quote from James Earl Jones in the movie "Field of Dreams" sums up baseball, the roadtrip and all that is good about the game...

The one constant through all the years, Ray, has been baseball. America has rolled by like an army of steamrollers. It's been erased like a blackboard, rebuilt, and erased again. But baseball has marked the time. This field, this game, is a part of our past, Ray. It reminds us of all that once was good, and that could be again. Oh people will come, Ray. People will most definitely come.
